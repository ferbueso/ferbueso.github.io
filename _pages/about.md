I work at the boundary where AI meets consequential decisions and I focus on making sure those decisions are safe, measurable, and trustworthy.

With a Master's in Artificial Intelligence for Biomedicine from University College London and a background in Biomedical Engineering, I've built my career at the intersection of technology, healthcare, and security. At CHANEL, I lead AI governance and security initiatives - designing risk frameworks, shaping AI standards aligned to the EU AI Act, ISO 42001, and NIST AI RMF, and helping global teams adopt AI responsibly across 50+ vendor relationships. My research at Great Ormond Street Hospital applied reinforcement learning to paediatric sepsis treatment optimization, evaluated on real patient data (a reminder that behind every model is a person whose outcome depends on getting it right). I've also spoken internationally on the cybersecurity challenges facing AI-enabled healthcare in Latin America, advocating for resilience as the defining capability of modern health systems. Whether in a boardroom, a research lab, or a conference stage, my goal is the same: bridge the gap between how powerful AI can be and how safely it can actually be deployed.

If you're working on problems at the crossroads of AI, healthcare, or security — [I'd love to connect](mailto:fbuesomedina@gmail.com).
